# Creating Wallets

This guide walks you through creating wallets in KaGet.

## Overview

KaGet supports two types of wallets:

| Type        | Description                  | Examples                        |
| ----------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **Cash**    | Physical money you carry     | Wallet, piggy bank, cash drawer |
| **Digital** | Money in electronic accounts | Bank account, e-wallet, PayPal  |

Both types work the same way in KaGet—the distinction helps you organize and identify your accounts.

## Creating a Wallet

1. From the dashboard, tap **Add Wallet** (or the `+` button)
2. Enter a **name** for your wallet (e.g., "Main Bank Account", "Cash")
3. Select the **type**: Cash or Digital
4. (Optional) Enter an **initial balance**
5. Tap **Create**

## Initial Balance

You can create a wallet with or without an initial balance:

### With Initial Balance

If your wallet already has money, enter the current amount. KaGet will create an "Opening Balance" record to track this.

> **Example:** You're adding your bank account that has $1,500. Enter `1500` as the initial balance. KaGet creates the wallet and adds an "Opening Balance" record of $1,500.

### Without Initial Balance

If you're starting fresh or will add the balance later, leave the initial balance empty (or enter `0`). You can add money later by recording income.

## What Happens Next

After creating a wallet:

- The wallet appears on your dashboard
- The balance shows your initial balance (or $0)
- You can start recording transactions
- You can transfer money to/from other wallets

## Tips

- **Be specific with names** — "Chase Checking" is better than "Bank"
- **Match real accounts** — Create one wallet per real-world account for accurate tracking
- **Start with your main accounts** — You can always add more wallets later
